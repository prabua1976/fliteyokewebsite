Aeronautics for young minds

The best mind to learn aeronautics is a young mind. You will be amazed at what they can achieve. All they need is encouragement, support and a structured learning platform tuned to their level

Inter Disciplinary Learning

With the right trigger, young minds easily learn and appreciate the link between many knowledge areas including science, mathematics, electronics (avionics), weather systems, communication systems, navigation and much more.

Hands-On Experiential Learning

By learning through experience, young minds assimilate advanced topics quickly and easily.

Hands-On

Children learn faster by DOING. While we give them a brief overview of the basics of flight, forces that act on an aircraft, the control surfaces that help the pilot manouvre the aricraft, they learn so much sitting on the cockpit simulator

Inter-Disciplinary

When young minds learn aeronautics, they actually end up learning science, mathematics, weather systems, communication technology, navigation...all in one package.

Learning is Fun

Children always look-up when they hear an aircraft fly above their heads. When they learn something they like, they learn faster and they have fun doing so.

ABOUT US

We are a father and son duo passionate about flying. What started as a simple hobby gave us plenty of learning opportunity and great connections. We interact, learn from and work closely with a lot of professionals in this field including pilots, aeronautical engineers, professors and senior managers. Now my child flies a variety of models (quads, electric fixed wing planes, nitro planes...) and flies many routes on the flight simulator. We constantly participate in college projects and MIT (Chennai) helped us realize that a kid so young can compete at par with other engineers. Children have so much potential that we as parents have to use our time/resource as the key to unlock them.

  • Our interests include RC Flying, Aero-Modelling and Flight Simulation.
  • We envision spreading this interest to others and more specifically young minds.
  • We are a regular visitor at Aero India. My son is a big fan of Surya Kiran.
  • We do not miss a single opportunity to learn something new about flying.
  • We keep connecting with professionals and organizations in aeronautics industry.
